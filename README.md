## 3. Compare and contrast Agile and Waterfall methodologies. What are the main advantages and disadvantages of each?
The agile method is flexible and iterative,suitable for changing requirements but less predictable.On the contrary the waterfall is linear and structured, good for stable projects but not flexible. agile involves continuous customer feedback, while Waterfall has limited customer interaction until the end.

## 4. In what types of projects might Agile be more beneficial than Waterfall, and vice versa? Can you provide examples of each?
Agile is more beneficial for projects with evolving requirements and a need for flexibility. Examples include software development for startups, where customer needs and market conditions can change rapidly, and creative projects like game development, where iterative testing and feedback are crucial.
Waterfall is better suited for projects with well-defined, stable requirements and a clear sequence of tasks. Examples include construction projects, where each phase must be completed before moving to the next, and manufacturing processes, where the steps are predictable and repeatable.

## 6. How does defining the project scope contribute to successful project planning? What is a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS), and why is it useful?
Defining the scope helps to define the limits of the project outlines the deliverables to the clients.
A Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) is a diagram that shows the connections between the objectives, measurable milestones, and deliverables for a project. It useful because it helps to  make a project more manageable and quantifiable by breaking up the work into smaller and easy to perform tasks.

## 7. What are the benefits of developing a detailed project schedule, and how can Gantt charts assist in this process?
A detailed project schedule is essential for effective project management. It tracks progress, boosts productivity, and minimizes delays and costs. Clear schedules improve stakeholder communication and resource management. Gantt charts help by visually representing timelines, task durations, and dependencies, making it easier to track progress and adjust schedules
